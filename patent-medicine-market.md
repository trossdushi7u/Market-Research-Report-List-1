<p><p>The patent medicine market comprises two primary types of patents: product patents and process patents. Product patents protect the specific formulation or composition of a drug, granting exclusive rights to the inventor for a defined period. In contrast, process patents safeguard the methods or techniques used to manufacture a medicine, allowing companies to monopolize innovative production processes. Both types foster innovation and R&D while influencing market dynamics, pricing strategies, and competition within the pharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
