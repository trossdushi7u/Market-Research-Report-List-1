<p><p>Contactless ticketing smart card technologies consist primarily of two market types: memory cards and microcontroller cards. Memory cards store data without processing capabilities, offering basic functions such as ticket validation and fare storage. They are typically more cost-effective and easier to implement. In contrast, microcontroller cards incorporate processing units, enabling advanced functionalities like secure data encryption, multi-application support, and interactive features. This versatility allows for enhanced security and integration into broader smart transit ecosystems, catering to evolving customer needs.</p></p>
