<p><p>District heating and cooling systems provide centralized temperature regulation for buildings within a specific area. District heating involves the distribution of hot water or steam generated from a central plant to provide warmth. It efficiently meets residential and commercial heating needs, reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Conversely, district cooling utilizes chilled water from a central source to cool buildings, optimizing energy use during hot weather. Both systems enhance energy efficiency, lower operational costs, and support sustainable urban development through shared resources.</p></p>
