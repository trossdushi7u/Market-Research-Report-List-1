<p><p>The skim milk market consists primarily of two types: pasteurized skim milk and ultra-high temperature (UHT) skim milk. Pasteurized skim milk undergoes heating to eliminate bacteria, providing a shorter shelf life and requiring refrigeration. In contrast, UHT skim milk is subjected to higher temperatures, allowing it to be shelf-stable for extended periods without refrigeration. Both types cater to different consumer preferences for freshness and convenience, impacting distribution strategies and market demand in the dairy industry.</p></p>
