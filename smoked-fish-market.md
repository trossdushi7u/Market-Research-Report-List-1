<p><p>The smoked fish market is generally divided into two main types: hot smoked fish and cold smoked fish. Hot smoked fish is subjected to higher temperatures during the smoking process, resulting in a fully cooked product with a flaky texture and robust flavor. Conversely, cold smoked fish is smoked at lower temperatures, allowing for a more delicate texture and a subtler taste, while remaining raw. Both types cater to unique consumer preferences, influencing their market demand and culinary applications.</p></p>
