<p><p>The disposable medical textiles market is primarily categorized into non-woven and woven fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are produced by bonding fibers together through various methods, creating lightweight, absorbent, and fluid-resistant materials ideal for surgical gowns, masks, and drapes. Woven fabrics, made by interlacing yarns, offer durability and strength, often used in applications like sterilization wraps and high-quality surgical textiles. Both types serve essential roles in infection control and patient safety in healthcare settings.</p></p>
