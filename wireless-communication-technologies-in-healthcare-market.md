<p><p>Wireless communication technologies in healthcare encompass various systems that enhance patient care and operational efficiency. Bluetooth is used for short-range device connectivity, facilitating monitoring and data transfer. Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) enables accurate tracking of equipment and medication. Ultra Wide Band (UWB) supports precise location tracking in facilities. WLAN (Wireless Local Area Network) and WWAN (Wireless Wide Area Network) provide broader connectivity for mobile health applications. Wi-Fi offers high-speed internet access, while Li-Fi provides wireless communication through light, enhancing data transfer in clinical environments.</p></p>
