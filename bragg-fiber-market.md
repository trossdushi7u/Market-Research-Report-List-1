<p><ul><li>Hollow Core</li><li>Solid Core</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Bragg Fiber market can be categorized into two main types: hollow core and solid core fibers. Hollow core fibers utilize an air-filled core surrounded by a photonic bandgap structure, offering low loss and high bandwidth, making them ideal for telecommunications and sensing applications. In contrast, solid core fibers consist of a solid glass core that provides excellent light guiding properties and high durability, commonly used in traditional telecommunications and industrial systems. Both types serve distinct needs in fiber optic technology.</p></p>
